What Are Coral Reefs?

Coral reefs are some of the most diverse ecosystems in the world. Coral polyps, the animals primarily responsible for building reefs, can take many forms: large reef-building colonies, graceful flowing fans, and even small, solitary organisms. Thousands of species of corals have been discovered; some live in warm, shallow, tropical seas and others in the cold, dark depths of the ocean.

Threats to Coral Reefs

Coral reefs are facing various threats including climate change, ocean acidification, sea warming, coral bleaching, pollution, overfishing, destructive fishing, destruction of habitats, coastal development, sedimentation, plastic pollution, oil spills, invasive species, disease, chemicals in sunscreen, mining of corals, impacts of tourism, boat anchoring, marine debris, nutrient runoff, sewage outfall, toxic algal blooms, and stress from global warming.

How We Can Help

We can help coral reefs through reduced carbon emissions, reef-safe sunscreens, reduced plastic use, encouraging sustainable fishing, ocean habitats conservation, anti-pollution, corals planting, water saving, participating in reef clean-up events, educating others, mobilizing for policies, reducing wastage, using eco-friendly products, and contributing to ocean conservation organizations so that coral reefs can last long.
