1.What is Overfishing

Overfishing takes place when too many fish are being caught more rapidly than they can replenish themselves, reducing fish populations. This may harm ocean habitats, destabilize food webs, and destroy habitats like coral reefs. It may eliminate species over time and affect global seafood supplies. There needs to be sustainable fishing to protect ocean life and ensure future fish populations.

2.The Environmental Impacts of Overfishing

Overfishing hurts marine ecosystems by taking away fish stocks, disrupting food chains and reducing biodiversity. It also destroys habitats, including coral reefs, through bottom trawling and other destructive fishing methods. When there are no fish, ecosystems are disrupted, and other creatures are damaged, and it is harder for the environment to bounce back.

3. Solutions and Sustainable Practices for Combating Overfishing

Catch limits, marine protected areas (MPAs), and sustainable fishing are the significant solutions against overfishing. MPAs allow for a recovery of the ecosystem, whereas catch limits prevent over-exploitation. Support of sustainable methods of fishing as well as seafood consumers' decisions regarding responsibly fished seafood is essential for retaining fish stocks as well as the healthy ocean for the long-term.
